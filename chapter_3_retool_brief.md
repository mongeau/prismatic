# Chapter 3: "The Diver" — Retool Brief

**From: Pax (Architect)**
**To: Claude (Writer)**

## The Core Problem
The current Chapter 3 has excellent worldbuilding ("silence shaped like a room") but suffers from three specific issues. First, the feral thread drops — Elara observes the seed pod from a distance instead of obsessing over it. Second, there are two physical staging errors regarding the pod's location and how it gets shocked. Third, the apparition of Mira acts too much like a video game quest NPC ("Not yet. You need the ship first") instead of a terrifying dimensional echo. 

We need to fix the staging, dial up the feral possessiveness, and make the vision ambiguous and unsettling.

## The Purpose Test
1. **What is the single major revelation?** The dimensional current isn't just theoretical; it's a navigable ocean, and the Chronos Nexus is the ship that can sail it.
2. **Which character's specific wound is activated?** Elara's fear that she is going insane vs. her terrifying hope that she isn't. The moment she chooses to believe the impossible over Archive logic is the moment she crosses the point of no return.
3. **What is the sensory anchor?** The physical weight and heat of the preservation case against her ribs, and the structural groaning of the dying ship.

## The Fixes (Thematic & Staging)

### 1. The Feral Proximity Fix
**Current:** "Elara did not touch it. Had not touched it since placing it in the case."
**The Fix:** A feral mother doesn't put the case on a shelf. She sleeps with it. She wears it. The preservation case is strapped to her ribs inside her thermal layers, under her pressure suit. She adjusts its temperature manually even though the seed is dormant, because *what if*. This proximity is how we bridge the pocket-carrying of Ch 2 to the current timeline.

### 2. The Electrical Discharge Staging Fix
**Current:** An electrical arc hits her suit and somehow shocks a pod in an "inner pocket" without cooking Elara.
**The Fix:** The electrical discharge doesn't hit the pod. It hits the *suit's external diagnostic port*, which surges the suit's internal thermal regulators. The thermal spike hits the preservation case strapped to her ribs. The pod doesn't wake up because of electricity; it wakes up because the sudden, intense heat mimics the extraction chamber environment. 

### 3. The Apparition Fix
**Current:** "Not yet," the child said in a voice that wasn't Mira's. "You need the ship first." (Too helpful, too clear).
**The Fix:** The apparition shouldn't give instructions. It should say something only Mira would know, but stripped of context, making it terrifying. A fragment of a memory. A sensory detail. *Pax recommends:* The apparition says something about the temperature of the water, or the color of the ceiling in the quarantine room. It doesn't tell Elara what to do. It just proves it's real. Elara deduces the need for the ship on her own from the navigation array data.

### 4. The Melodrama Fixes
**Current:** "Elara crushed the scanner under her boot."
**The Fix:** Elara is precise. She doesn't throw tantrums. She carefully, methodically unspools the scanner's power cell and drops it into the recycler. A quiet, permanent choice.
**Current:** "Something that would tear holes in reality to find its way home."
**The Fix:** Cut this movie-trailer line entirely. End on the quiet realization that she is going to have to recruit a crew.
