# PRISMATIC: EDITORIAL RETOOL ROADMAP

This document tracks the surgical retooling of the existing manuscript (Chapters 1-12) before we draft the new chapters (Part 3 & 4). 

**The Goal:** We need to align the existing text with the new thematic core (feral motherhood, the Archives as predator) and strip out the five systemic prose issues (exposition creep, em-dash addiction, Yukimoto/Nyara blur, narrator intrusion, the "What is that?" pattern).

## RETOOL ORDER

We will retool in this specific order. Mike will feed the provided prompt to Claude for each chapter, one at a time.

### 1. Chapter 8: "The Nexus" (Highest Priority)
**Why:** This is the weakest chapter. It suffers from heavy exposition creep and slow velocity during the ship theft.
**The Fix:** 
- Strip the 3-paragraph ship description.
- Make the escape kinetic (Scalzi style).
- Describe the dimensional current entirely through physical symptoms (nausea, vertigo, bleeding), not quantum physics lectures.
- Keep the "STATE YOUR INTENTION" sequence intact.

### 2. Chapter 1: "The Extraction" (The Feral Injection & Staging Fix)
**Why:** The chapter is already excellent, but we need to seed the feral possessiveness so the ending feels inevitable. We also need to fix critical physical staging errors.
**The Fix:**
- **Thematic:** Elara pocketing the seed pod isn't calculated theft; it's a reflexive, animalistic action. Her hand closes around it before her brain catches up. Remove any reflection. The body acts, the mind follows.
- **Staging Flag (Critical):** The suspension gel and the pillow cannot coexist in the same physical space. Mira is described as floating in gel (line 5) but reaching under a pillow (line 22). The membrane, transfer slot, and gel also need to be reconciled as a single coherent environment. **Claude decides the solution.** Do not lose the ozone smell or the sensory specificity of the extraction environment.

### 3. Chapter 2: "The Harvester" (The Mask of Competence)
**Why:** Dependency from Chapter 1. If Elara is feral in Ch 1, she can't be perfectly professional here.
**The Fix:**
- Frame her Archive work as *performing* competence while something underneath is already broken. The feral thread must stay alive under the surface.

### 4. Chapter 3: "The Diver" (The Proximity & Staging Fix)
**Why:** Feral mothers don't observe from a distance. We also need to fix physical logic errors regarding the seed pod's location.
**The Fix:**
- **Thematic:** Change Elara refusing to touch the pod. She should sleep with the preservation case against her body. She adjusts its temperature manually even though the seed is dormant, because *what if*.
- **Staging Flag:** The pod jumps from bare-hand pocket (Ch. 1-2) to a preservation case (Ch. 3) with no transition. Also: an electrical discharge cannot reach an "inner pocket" through a sealed pressure suit without also injuring Elara. **Claude decides how to resolve both.** The pod's physical journey must be trackable chapter to chapter.

### 5. Chapter 5: "The Conman" (The Body Language)
**Why:** We need to see the possessiveness through someone else's eyes.
**The Fix:**
- When Elara shows Cassius the pod, her body language must change. She angles her body to protect it. Cassius recognizes the posture of someone who would kill to keep what they hold.

### 6. Chapter 9: "Fractures" (The Priority)
**Why:** The final feral seed before the jump.
**The Fix:**
- During the dimensional sickness, the ship's systems fluctuate. Elara checks the pod's preservation case *before* checking on her crew. Mez notices this disproportionate urgency and stays silent.

### 7. Chapter 10: "The Arrival" (The Mirror Match & Staging Fix)
**Why:** The worldbuilding overwhelms the character tension. This is where we introduce Original Elara as the physical antagonist for Part 4.
**The Fix:**
- Restructure the city tour to escalate: wonder → unease → horror.
- **Thematic Injection:** Original Elara is a mother who *took the deal*. She has a constructed Mira. We need one moment during her Convergence Protocol speech where her mask slips—a gesture too gentle for a director—showing the feral mother underneath. She isn't an authority figure; she's a threat because she'll burn worlds to keep her fake child.
- **Staging Flag:** The opening repeats a description block verbatim from the end of Chapter 9 — cut the duplicate. The reversed airlock physics (effect precedes cause) are stated but not physically staged in a way the reader can feel. **Claude decides how to make the temporal wrongness visceral.**

### 8. Chapter 4: "The Equation" (Nyara's Setup)
**Why:** We changed Nyara's ultimate sacrifice in Part 4 (she embraces the "error"). We need to set that up.
**The Fix:**
- Emphasize her father's belief that "approximation is murder."
- Show Nyara's rigid reliance on perfect math as a trauma response. She needs to be terrified of irrationality so her final choice to embrace it lands harder.

### 9. Chapter 12: "The Summons" (The Transition & Staging Fix)
**Why:** This is the bridge into Part 3. We need to clarify the nature of the voice Elara hears.
**The Fix:**
- Clarify that the voice Elara hears in the crystal isn't Mira trapped in a box; it's the *pattern* of Mira echoing in the current.
- Elara's reaction should be terrifyingly single-minded. She doesn't care about the physics; she just wants to catch the echo.
- **Staging Flag:** The seed crystal's physical journey from pocket → pedestal → Yukimoto → navigation core has gaps. The line "hadn't been in her pocket since the integration" needs the preceding chapters to show each handoff. **Claude decides how to track it.**
