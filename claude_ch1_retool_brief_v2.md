### The Problem
Elara pocketing the seed pod currently reads as a slightly calculated, rebellious choice. It needs to be a feral reflex. The Anti-Midnight-Library Rule starts here: she is not noble; she is a mother who will steal from the universe to keep a piece of her kid. 

### What Must Change
1. **The Feral Theft:** Make the theft of the seed pod animalistic. Her hand closes around it before her brain catches up. Remove any reflection about "professional obligation." The body acts, the mind justifies it later.
2. **The Staging Flag (Critical):** The suspension gel and the pillow cannot coexist in the same physical space. Mira is described as floating in gel but reaching under a pillow. The membrane, transfer slot, and gel need to be reconciled as a single coherent environment. 
   - **Your Call:** You decide how to fix this physical contradiction. 
   - **Do Not Cut:** You MUST preserve the ozone smell, the sensory specificity of the extraction environment, and the domestic intimacy of the moment (even if the "pillow" specifically has to change).

### What Must Stay
- The core dialogue between Elara and Mira ("Made you laugh," "Big fat Archive liar").
- The ending sequence where Elara leaves the room and experiences the physical sensation of grief.
- The overall pacing and emotional weight. Do not overwrite this; it's already close.

### The Purpose Test
Does this chapter establish Elara as someone who loves her daughter so fiercely that she would eventually let herself be torn apart across infinite dimensions to save her echo? If the theft feels like a calculated crime rather than a biological imperative, it fails the test.
